About Akorede Inclusion Foundation

Founded with deep compassion and conviction, Akorede Inclusion Foundation was born from the belief that every individual, regardless of their neurology, deserves dignity.

We are committed to raising awareness about autism and other disabilities, supporting individuals and families through advocacy, education, and community-driven initiatives. Our work reaches families in urban centers and rural communities alike, ensuring no one is left behind.

From training educators to empowering caregivers and championing inclusive policy, we take a holistic approach to building a Nigeria, and a world, where everyone can thrive.

Understanding Autism

What is Autism Spectrum Disorder?

Knowledge fosters acceptance. Learn what autism is, how to recognize early signs, and how to support individuals living with autism with dignity.

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurological condition that affects communication, behavior, and social interaction. It typically appears in early childhood and manifests differently in every individual — which is why we call it a "spectrum."

Autism is not a disease. It is a different way of experiencing the world. Individuals living with autism have unique strengths and perspectives that enrich our communities when given the right support and opportunity.

🔍

Early Signs

Delayed speech, limited eye contact, repetitive behaviors, or unusual sensory sensitivities may appear by age 2–3. Early intervention is key.

💡

Why Awareness Matters

Stigma and misinformation remain barriers to support. Awareness creates environments where individuals living with autism feel safe, accepted, and celebrated.

❤️

Supporting Individuals Living with Autism

Patient communication, sensory-friendly spaces, individualized education plans, and inclusive workplaces make a profound difference in quality of life.

Common Characteristics

  • Differences in verbal and non-verbal communication
  • Strong preference for routine and structure
  • Heightened or reduced sensitivity to sensory input
  • Intense, focused interests in specific subjects
  • Unique ways of learning and processing information
  • Social interaction differences — not deficits
  • Strengths in pattern recognition, memory, and attention to detail

Every individual living with autism is unique — the spectrum is not a line from "mild" to "severe"
